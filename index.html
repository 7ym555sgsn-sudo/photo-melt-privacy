PhotoMelt — Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 27, 2026

PhotoMelt (“the app,” “we,” “us”) helps contractors turn job-site photos into organized, phase-grouped time-lapse videos. This policy explains exactly what data the app handles and where it goes. We keep it short because the app does very little with your data.

What the app accesses

  • Your photos. With your permission, PhotoMelt reads photos from your library to find job-site images, group them by area and build phase, and assemble time-lapse videos. You choose what to import; the app does not scan or upload your library on its own.
  • Saving videos. With your permission, PhotoMelt saves the time-lapse videos it renders back to your photo library.

What gets sent off your device — and to whom

To classify a photo (is it a job site? what phase and area?), the app sends that image to our backend, a Supabase Edge Function we operate. That function holds our AI provider key and forwards the image to OpenAI for analysis, then returns a short text result (phase, area label, a one-line description) to the app.

  • Images are sent only when you run analysis, and only for that purpose.
  • Our backend does not store your images — it passes them to OpenAI, returns the result, and keeps nothing.
  • OpenAI processes the image to generate the analysis. Per OpenAI’s API terms, API content is not used to train their models, and is retained only briefly for abuse monitoring before deletion.

Anonymous device identifier

The app generates a random identifier for your install (a UUID stored on your device) and sends it with analysis requests so our backend can enforce a fair daily usage limit. This identifier is not tied to your name, email, Apple ID, contacts, or location, and is not used to track you across other apps or websites. Our backend stores only this random ID plus a per-day request count — never your photos or any personal information.

What we do NOT collect

  • No account or login.
  • No name, email, phone number, or address.
  • No location data.
  • No advertising identifiers, and no third-party analytics or ad SDKs.

Children

PhotoMelt is a business tool intended for use by adults and is not directed to children under 13.
